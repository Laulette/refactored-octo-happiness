If you want to compete in tournaments, make sure you know what's required of you first.

Have the right weapon.
Don't bring a smallsword to a rapiers-only tournament. Make sure your weapon meets all length, type, and other requirements.
Understand the rules.
Don't just know the rules: understand them. Different tournament organizers may have different rules, requirements, and expectations. Learn the rules well in advance and remember to spar using the tournament rules before you get anywhere near the tournament site. It'll give you a better chance of doing well, and it'll make the experience more enjoyable for everyone involved.
Be prepared to exhibit good sportsmanship.
Sometimes, judges make mistakes. Sometimes, your opponent is rude. Sometimes, you lose. Sometimes, you win. You should always act with grace and compassion during competitions. Tournaments can be very emotionally charged, and it improves the experience for everyone, including yourself, if you make a point of exhibiting good sportsmanship.
